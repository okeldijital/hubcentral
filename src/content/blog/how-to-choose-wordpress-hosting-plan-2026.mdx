**The most important factor when choosing a WordPress hosting plan is not the launch price — it’s the renewal price.**  
Every other decision flows from understanding what you’ll actually pay in month 13, not month one.

Most small teams choose wrong because they evaluate hosting like a one-time purchase rather than a recurring infrastructure commitment. This guide gives you the six questions that cut through the marketing noise and get you to the right plan for your actual situation.

## The 6 Key Questions You Must Ask Before Choosing a Plan

### 1. What Will I Actually Pay After the Introductory Period?
Every major host uses low introductory pricing to attract signups. The gap between intro and renewal pricing varies dramatically.

**What to do:** Before signing up, find the renewal pricing page (not the homepage promo). Calculate your 24-month total cost. The cheapest launch price is rarely the cheapest long-term.

### 2. What Is the Server’s Baseline Speed?
Your host’s server response time (TTFB) is the floor beneath every optimisation you apply. No plugin can fix a slow server.

**What good looks like in 2026:** Under 400–500ms TTFB.  
**What to do:** Run GTmetrix on a live site hosted by your shortlisted provider. If TTFB is over 500ms, fix your host before touching plugins.

### 3. Does the Plan Include Backups and Staging?
These two features tell you more about quality than almost anything else.

- **Backups:** Should be automatic and daily, stored off-server.  
- **Staging:** A copy of your live site for safe testing (essential for AI tools and major changes).

### 4. What Does Support Actually Look Like?
“24/7 support” means different things to different hosts.

- Live chat that actually resolves issues (Hostinger)  
- Phone support (GoDaddy)  
- Ticket-only (Namecheap)

Match the support model to the person who will contact them when something goes wrong.

### 5. Can This Plan Handle Growth Without a Painful Migration?
Your plan should match where your site is today and have a clear upgrade path for where it will be in 18 months.

**What to look for:** In-account upgrades (no full migration required).  
**What to avoid:** Hosts where the next tier is a completely different infrastructure.

### 6. Is This Host Ready for AI-Assisted Workflows?
In 2026 this matters.

Look for PHP 8.1+, reliable REST API performance, and staging environments for safely testing AI plugins.

## Plan Types Explained

### Shared Hosting — The Right Starting Point for Most Teams
Your site shares a server with others. Good for low-traffic sites and most small teams.

### Managed WordPress Hosting — The Practical Step Up
WordPress-optimised environment with automatic updates, built-in caching, and better support.

### VPS / Cloud Hosting — Only When You Need It
Dedicated resources for higher traffic or WooCommerce stores.

## Common Mistakes Small Teams Make

- Buying more than they need on day one  
- Choosing based on launch price instead of renewal price  
- Ignoring staging environments  
- Treating hosting as a “set and forget” decision

## Quick Decision Checklist
- Calculate your 24-month total cost  
- Check TTFB on a live site hosted by the provider  
- Confirm daily backups and staging are included  
- Match support model to your team’s comfort level  
- Verify upgrade path for future growth  
- Test on a short-term plan before committing long-term
