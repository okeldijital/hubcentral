## What to Avoid

1. Choosing purely by the lowest launch price — always check the renewal rate.  
2. Using basic shared hosting for any site that receives real traffic or paid ads.  
3. Ignoring page speed — slow hosting hurts both user experience and SEO.  
4. Skipping staging environments when testing changes or updates.  
5. Treating hosting as a “set and forget” decision — your site is the foundation for your business workflows.

## Quick WordPress Migration Checklist

- Export a full backup of your current site (files + database)  
- Use the new host’s migration tool (Hostinger offers free automated migration)  
- Test everything on a staging subdomain first  
- Update DNS only after the new site is working perfectly  
- Verify SSL and check Google Search Console after migration
