## How to Test Hosting Speed Yourself in 2026

Instead of relying on marketing claims, run your own quick tests. It takes less than 10 minutes and gives you honest data for your own situation.

**Simple test method:**
- Sign up for a short-term plan (most hosts offer 30-day money-back guarantees)
- Install a clean WordPress site with Astra theme (no extra plugins or CDN)
- Import a small starter template with 8–12 pages and a few images
- Use [GTmetrix](https://gtmetrix.com) (free) or Google PageSpeed Insights
- Choose a test server location close to your main audience
- Run the test 3–5 times at different times of day and average the results

**Key metrics to watch:**
- **TTFB** (Time to First Byte) – the most important hosting-specific number
- **LCP** (Largest Contentful Paint) – what Google uses for Core Web Vitals
- **Total Load Time** – real-world user experience

## What the Numbers Actually Mean for Small Teams

**TTFB is the hosting floor.** Everything else (caching, CDN, image optimisation) builds on top of it. A lower TTFB gives you more room before you hit performance problems.

**LCP is what Google cares about for rankings.** A host that keeps LCP comfortably under 2.5s on a clean site gives you headroom when you add real content and plugins.

**Consistency matters.** A host that is fast most of the time but occasionally slow creates a worse user experience than one that is reliably “good enough”.

**The speed gap compounds.** Every additional plugin, image, or database query makes the difference between hosts larger.
